PRE_Sections

​Considerations prior to updating of establishment details (added 3 June 2021)

The following should be considered prior to updating establishment details:

  • When an establishment notifies the Department of an administrative change (that is, a change of occupier, company name, address, or in some instances product specific changes), the Department will amend the establishment record in the system.
  • The establishment's amended details will appear on applicable export documentation including health certification from the date that the Department amends the establishment record
  • When relevant changes are made to an establishment's details, the Department will advise trading partners that maintain establishment lists of the change of details; however, the Department is unable to guarantee a timeframe as to when trading partners will update the establishment's overseas listing details they maintain. This means that there may be a period in which export certification issued by the Department does not match the trading partner's official published listing, which may result in clearance issues at the port of entry.
  • Establishments should be aware that their ability to export to some countries may be impacted during this transition period and may include issues with goods in transit.
  • Exporters and manufacturers are encouraged to check that their establishment's details are recorded accurately where they appear on trading partners' lists and to contact the Department if errors are found.

1.1 Establishment registration

PMA_EstablishmentRegistration

Establishment listing required for South KoreaYes

Establishments that manufacture dairy products for human consumption (excludes inedible products and pet food) for export to South Korea must be listed with South Korea's Ministry of Food and Drug Safety (MFDS).

To obtain a list of MFDS listed Australian establishments go to the MFDS searchable database.

How to apply for listing

MFDS has advised that new establishments seeking to export dairy products must submit a formal registration application through the Department.

Your application for listing with MFDS must include:

  • application form for registration of foreign establishment (PDF 160 KB) – requires all exporters to agree to permit MFDS to visit and inspect their establishment*
  • documentation which verifies regular management and supervision of the establishment by the Department (that is, most recent audit report)
  • a certificate of registration to export issued by the Department
  • a Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point (HACCP) certificate
  • a copy of the HACCP document/s for the product/s you intend to export to South Korea. This document should include the work process chart indicating all critical control points
  • the submission of all documents to dairyeggsfish@agriculture.gov.au

*MFDS has advised that, as part of the approval process, establishments may be subject to an on-site audit.

Note
Any audits will be coordinated through the Department.
Changes to a listed establishment

The manufacturing dairy establishment is required to submit a completed application form to notify the Department of any administrative changes (for example, change of occupier, company name, address or in some instances product specific changes). The Department will then amend the establishment record in the Department’s Establishment Register (ER) system.

The Department will then request MFDS make the amendments to their list of manufacturing dairy establishments. Establishments are required to provide the Department with the following documentation:

  • A current HACCP or Approved Arrangement certificate
  • The MFDS establishment listing application (PDF 84 KB)
Renewal of registration

MFDS has advised dairy establishment listings do not expire. However, if a dairy establishment exports other non-dairy products (such as agricultural produce, processed food, food additivities, health functional food) and has applied for registration as a food facility, they should renew their registration online with MFDS.

To renew your registration visit Application for renewing registration of foreign food facility. For more information on the renewal process, refer to the step-by-step guide (PDF 160 KB).

Exceptions (ships stores)

Export certification is not issued for ships stores as the Export Control (Milk and Milk Products) Rules 2021 do not apply. Ships stores are milk and milk products on ships or aircraft for the use of passengers and crew or for the service of the ships or aircraft on a voyage from Australia.

1.3 Prohibited products

PMA_ProhibitedProducts
Edible products and edible by-products

South Korea introduced new legislation which came into force on 4 August 2016 entitled 'the Special Act on Imported Food Safety Management' (the Act). The Act only applies to livestock products for human consumption (excludes inedible products and pet food).

The Act establishes categories of livestock products which must be approved by South Korea before being eligible for export from Australia. Livestock products with an established history of trade are eligible for continued export to South Korea. For livestock products which South Korea considers as 'new trade', South Korea will require an import sanitation assessment and bilateral export certification to be negotiated prior to commencing trade.

PPHC_MilkMilkProducts

Milk and milk products of bovine origin are defined as prescribed goods under the Export Control Act. Production of these products for export is regulated under the Export Control (Milk and Milk Products) Rules 2021.

Milk and milk products manufactured from non-bovine species, for example buffalo, goat and sheep, may also need to be regulated through the Export Control (Milk and Milk Products) Rules 2021, though this is only required when export registration and health certification from the Department is required by an importing country.

We are aware that the Republic of Korea (South Korea) has a requirement for export certification for ice cream, goat milk infant formula and goat milk powder (refer to section 5.1.1).

Major changes notified in G/SPS/N/KOR/457 that relate to milk and milk products
  • Lactic acid bacteria will not be included in bacterial counts in products in which lactic acid bacteria are added.
  • The coliforms standard for ice cream and ice cream mix are amended to: 10vcfu/ml, n=5, c=2, m=10, M=100
  • Nutrient specifications for infant formula and follow-up formula are amended to allow the addition of selenium
  • Maximum reference levels in specifications of linoleic acid, vitamin A/D/C/B1/B2, niacin, folic acid, pantothenic acid, potassium, chloride, magnesium, iron, iodine, copper and manganese in infant formula powder are revised
  • Specifications of linoleic acid, vitamin B1/B2/B6/K1/E, niacin, folic acid, pamtothenic acid, calcium, phosphate, magnesium, iodine, zinc and manganese in infant formula powder and linoleic acid in follow-up formula powder are revised
  • The name for nicotinic acid is changed to niacin
  • The sample number and amount permitted of Enterobacter sakazakii in infant formula powder is amended.

3.1 Technical grade and pharmaceutical products

PPNHC_TechnicalGradePharmaceuticalProducts

Dairy based technical grade products (not for human consumption) are non-prescribed goods. When a dairy based technical grade product is prepared in an export registered establishment, it must meet the requirements of the Export Control (Milk and Milk Products) Rules 2021. Technical grade products must be:

  • Clearly identified as technical grade product and where applicable, labelled as 'for trial/ testing purposes only – not for human consumption'
  • Segregated from, and not contaminate milk and milk products that are for human consumption.

The production of technical grade products in an export registered establishment must be included in the company's approved arrangement (AA).

4. Operational requirements

OR_OperationalRequirements

4.1 Microbial and residue sampling

OR_MicrobialResidueSampling

Microbial sampling of milk and milk products can be met through the Export Control (Milk and Milk Products) Rules 2021.

Exporters should be aware that South Korea has specifications for dairy products that include microbiological criteria (refer to Section 2.1) and consignments may be tested on arrival to check compliance with these criteria (refer to Section 1.4 for details).

In general, South Korean regulations do not allow for food poisoning bacteria including Salmonella spp., Staphylococcus aureus, Vibrio parahaemolyticus, Clostridium perfringens, Listeria monocytogenes and E.coli O157:H7 to be detected in foods that have been pasteurised, sterilised or are to be consumed without further processing or cooking. However, South Korea has recently amended requirements for cheese to allow very low levels of Staphylococcus aureus to be present in cheese (refer to Section 2.1).

South Korea ​residue standards

Maximum residue limits (MRLs) or import tolerances for residues of specific agricultural and veterinary chemicals approved for use in Australia may not exist in South Korea or may be established at levels lower than those established in Australia. Maximum Limits (MLs) for contaminants may also vary between South Korea and Australia.

Food business operators should note that:

  • These differences can be interrogated case-by-case by considering the South Korea standards and the Australian standards.
  • Any detection of a residue above the South Korea ​MRLs/MLs (as applicable) at the border may result in rejection of the consignment.
Note
Importing country residue standards can usually be found via the official webpages of their competent authority. Links to a limited number of export markets and Australia are provided on the DAFF website.

5. Documentation requirements

DR_DocumentationRequirements

5.1 Milk and milk products

DR_MilkMilkProducts
Milk and milk products that are prescribed under Australia's export rules
  1. are milk or milk products derived from a bovine animal; and
  2. are intended to be exported as food.
Milk and milk products that are not prescribed under Australia's export rules

Milk and milk products which do not meet the criteria under the Export Control (Milk and Milk Products) Rules 2021 are classified by Australia as non-prescribed goods. It is the responsibility of the exporter to confirm with the importing country if there are any specific requirements for the import of these products.

Examples of non-prescribed goods are:

  1. ice cream
  2. cheesecakes, Bavarian desserts and similar desserts
  3. colostrum
  4. milk or milk products encased in pastry or a fruit or vegetable product
  5. milk products where milk or milk products are not the major component
  6. milk or milk products obtained from a non-bovine animal
  7. milk or milk products that are animal food or pharmaceutical material
  8. liquid milk and milk products exported in a consignment of not more than 10 litres
  9. milk or milk products exported in a consignment of not more than 10 kilograms
  10. milk or milk products in the form of a tablet or capsule
  11. milk or milk products for export to New Zealand.

Further information on exporting non-prescribed goods from Australia is available on the Department's website.

In some cases, an importing country requires specific health certification and government oversight for what would typically be non-prescribed milk and milk products. When required, these products become prescribed under export legislation and must meet all export and importing country requirements. This includes, the products being manufactured and stored at an export registered establishment and must have a valid export permit and health certificate for each consignment. Guidance on export registration can be found at Dairy export registered establishments.

Additional labelling requirements

South Korean authorities require the following details on product labels and may inspect labelling for compliance:

  • Liquid milk (including UHT milk) must be labelled with the manufacturing date (not in code) and the use-by date. This will be subject to audit.
  • Details of importer, distributor, exporter and manufacturer are required on product labels. If the exporter and manufacturer are the same then the information need only be shown once, likewise with the importer and distributor.
  • The country of origin is required on all labels, including inner wrappers.
  • Products containing more than 0.2 grams per serving of trans fatty acids require trans fats to be identified on labels.
  • Milk products must be labelled, according to the type of processed milk products i.e. milk, fortified milk, restored milk, or lactic acid bacteria added milk.
  • Fortified milk products must contain at least 2,000 IU and 400 IU per litre respectively of Vitamins A and D.
  • Fortified milk products must identify the name and content of the component that was fortified.
  • Products containing lactic acid bacteria must identify the sort and number of bacteria.
  • Milk and milk products that are heat treated must indicate either "Pasteurised" or "Sterilised" along with the temperature and duration of pasteurisation or sterilisation.
    • Long-time low temperature pasteurisation and short-time high temperature pasteurisation can be labelled as "pasteurisation."
  • Products in which milk fat is reduced and standardised must be labelled as "standardised product", "standardised milk" or "standardisation", etc. along with the quantity of milk fat.

  • No food components shall be used as the product name.
Lactose

  • Labels must indicate the ratio of crude proteins, amino acid nitrogen and casein phosphopeptides.
Formulated milk products
  • Formulated milk products must be labelled according to the type of processed milk products; for example, formulated milk powder, formulated milk, the formulated milk for a growth period, other formulated milk powder, other formulated milk.
  • Formulated milk products for premature infants must be labelled as "for premature infants."
  • Products must indicate feeding methods for babies and infants.
  • No food components shall be used as the product name.
  • Products containing 1 milligram of iron per 100 kilocalorie may be labelled as "iron enriched formulated milk."
  • Formulated milk powder and formulated milk must indicate such notice as "Breast milk is the best food for babies" with letters the size of which is bigger than 12 points.
Ice cream
  • Ice cream must be labelled according to the type of processed milk products; for example, ice cream, ice milk, sherbet, low-fat ice cream, non-milk fat ice cream.
  • Labels must indicate if the ice cream product contains lactic acid bacteria and fermented milk.
  • Fermented milk products containing lactic acid bacteria must indicate if the sort and number of bacteria.
  • Ice cream or ice milk products must indicate the quantity of milk fat,
  • Low-fat ice cream must indicate the quantity of fat.
  • Products containing lactic acid bacteria must indicate the sort and number of bacteria.
  • Actual weight of an ice cream can be indicated in volume.
Ice cream powder products
  • Ice cream powder products must be labelled according to the type of processed milk products; for example, ice cream powder, ice milk powder, sherbet powder, non-fat milk ice cream powder.
  • Ice cream powder or ice milk powder must indicate the quantity of milk fat.
  • Products containing lactic acid bacteria must indicate the sort and number of bacteria.
  • Fermented milk products containing lactic acid bacteria must indicate ifthe sort and number of bacteria. The amount of water added per the unit weight of the product must be indicated. (The recommended amount of added water is twice the weight of product.)
Ice cream mix products
  • Ice cream mix products must be labelled according to the type of processed milk products; for example, ice cream mix, ice milk mix, sherbet mix, low-fat ice cream mix, non-fat milk ice cream mix.
  • Products containing lactic acid bacteria must indicate the sort and number of bacteria.
  • Fermented milk products containing lactic acid bacteria must indicate if the sort and number of bacteria. Ice cream mix or ice milk mix products must indicate the quantity of milk fat while low-fat ice cream mix products shall indicate the quantity of fat.
  • Ice cream mix products must be labelled according to the heat treatment methods, etc as "pasteurised product" or "sterilised product."
